Dealing with the new Coronavirus: Best practices

Dealing with the new Coronavirus may seem overwhenlming and difficult, but the truth is everyone can contribute to fight against this deadly pandemic.There are a few things that we can do to help our health care system from collapsing and to protect our communities. Below are a few things that you can do to make a difference in the lives of people you may not know;

Social Distancing: It adhere to deliberately maintaining a distance of upto 6 feet from people in order to stop spreading the virus. This means going out to crowded places for pleasure such as restaurants, parks and other recreational activities should be avoided at all costs. Maintaining social distance will help to show down the spread of illness, ultimately protecting lives and reducing the burden on the health care system.

Self Quarantine: If a person has travelled from a place that has confirmed patients of Covid-19, or it is possible that he/she has been exposed to the Virus due to an infected person around them, it is recommended for them to isolate themselves for 15-days at least. The symptoms of Coronavirus can be seen within this period. This helps in reducing exposure of others to the illness. Once the period of self-quarantine ends, it is better to consult the healthcare providers about going back to normal routine.

Isolation: If a person has symptoms such as cough, fever or sore throat, then he/she should refrain from going out in public, instead staying at home or in a healthcare facility and limiting social interaction would be the best choice. Special protective equipment and strict procedures/protocols are used to take care of patients suffering from Covid-19. This will reduce the spread of the disease to others.

Flattening the Curve: This refers to being careful and taking precautions in order to avoid a lot of people conducting the new Coronavirus, so that the helthcare system does not collapse; hospitals have beds, supplies and availability of doctors to treat patients i.e Flattening the Curve. If a huge number of people fall sick, it would be difficult for doctors to treat so many patients at the same time, especially when treatment of Covid-19 requires numerous protocols and safety measures.
